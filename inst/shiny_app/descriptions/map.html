Summary:
The map panel can display the number of permits, number of items, churn rate, recruitment rate, or participation rate.

Churn rate is the number of customers lost after the current year divided by the total number of customers in the current year. Lost customers are defined as those who do not purchase in the next 5 permit years.

Recruitment rate is the number of recruits in the current year divided by the total number of customers the previous year. Recruits are defined as an individual that did not purchase in the 5 previous permit years.

Participation rate is defined as the percent of the population purchasing at least one permit. Population estimates are 5-year estimates from the 2013-2017 American Community Survey (ACS).

Grouping Variables:
The primary variable is calculate separately for each group. The main grouping variable is either county or state depending on the selected reporting region.The subplot variable drop down menu can be used to specify additional grouping variables.

 Tips:
To remove "(Missing)" group, activate filter by selecting all values. For example, check boxes for "Female" and "Male" on gender filter to remove entries with missing values for gender attribute.

Instead of selecting a subplot variable, try setting the filters on the sidebar and downloading a plot from the "Save plot" tab. Saved plots may be easier to compare because the colors are scaled to each plot.

 Caution:
The county and state variables are defined in the database. Check with your database administrator to see if these variables are simply the most recent information or if they can change over time as customers move.
